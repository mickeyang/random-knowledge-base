# gemini response

It is incredibly easy to get "AI fatigue" when you are bouncing between four heavy-hitting platforms. When every tool has a different sidebar, you end up wasting half your time just hunting down that one amazing answer you got three days ago. 

To fix this, we need to transform your chaotic tabs into an organized system based on two principles: **Strict Routing** (knowing exactly *which* AI to use for what) and a **Central Ledger** (knowing exactly *where* to find it later).

Here is a system and workflow designed to streamline your AI use.

---

## Phase 1: The "Who Does What" Matrix
Stop treating them all as general search engines. Give each AI a specific job description in your head so you automatically know which one to open.

| AI Tool | Core Superpower | Best Used For... |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **OpenAI ChatGPT** | Advanced logic, creative writing, and general conceptual brainstorming. | Brainstorming essay outlines, polishing English copy, and high-level coding logic. |
| **Google Gemini** | Real-time web research and visual data mapping. | Quick fact-checking, deep-diving current events, and turning data into charts. |
| **Zhipu Z-AI (GLM)** | Massive document parsing (200K context window) and deep technical reasoning. | Uploading long PDFs/academic papers, heavy-duty coding debugging, and deep technical study. |
| **Tencent Yuanbao** | Deep Tencent ecosystem access (WeChat Official Accounts) and agent tasks. | Finding localized Chinese trends, searching specific WeChat industry reports, and automated agent workflows. |

---

## Phase 2: The "Central Ledger" System
To stop forgetting where your answers are, you need **one external home** for your knowledge. Do not rely on the history sidebars of four different websites.

Choose *one* tool to act as your Master Log (Notion, Obsidian, Apple Notes, or a simple markdown file). Every time you have a major session, log it in a simple table like this:

> **The AI Inbox Template:**
> * **Topic/Project:** [e.g., History Essay / Python Script]
> * **AI Tool Used:** [e.g., Zhipu Z-AI]
> * **Status/Takeaway:** [e.g., Solved. Saved the final prompt and code snippet here.]
> * **Direct Link:** [Paste the shared chat link if the platform supports it]

*Tip: If you use Notion, you can set up a simple database with tags. If you prefer low-tech, just keep a single pinned note on your phone/computer.*

---

## Phase 3: The Ideal Workflow Loop

Whenever a new question pops into your head, follow this quick 3-step loop:

1. **Route (2 seconds):** Look at your Matrix. Is this a deep document analysis question? (Go to Zhipu). Is it about a trending topic in China? (Go to Yuanbao). 
2. **Name the Chat Immediately (5 seconds):** The moment you open the correct AI, type your prompt, and get an answer, **rename the chat session** in the sidebar. Instead of letting it default to *"How to write a..."*, manually change it to something scannable, like `[PROJ] Game Code V1` or `[STUDY] Bio Exam`. 
3. **The "High-Value" Export (30 seconds):** If the AI gives you a breakthrough answer, **do not leave it in the tab**. Copy the final clean summary or code, drop it into your Central Ledger under the correct project name, and close the AI tab. Treat the AI as a workspace, and your Ledger as your archive.

By moving your final answers out of the AI platforms and into your own notes, you eliminate the need to go digging through four different history sidebars.
